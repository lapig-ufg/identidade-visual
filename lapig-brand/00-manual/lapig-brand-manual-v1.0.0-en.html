01 — Brand Platform

Who We Are

The strategic foundations that guide every communication and visual identity decision at LAPIG.

Purpose

Turn geospatial data into accessible knowledge for the protection and sustainable use of Brazilian and global landscapes.

Mission

Produce world-class research in remote sensing and GIS, focused on pastureland monitoring and land-use dynamics, generating open data that informs environmental policy, the climate agenda, and evidence-based decision-making.

Vision

Be the global benchmark for territorial intelligence on pasturelands and land use, recognized for scientific rigor, technological innovation, and the measurable impact of our products on public policy and carbon markets.

Values

  • Scientific rigor at every stage, from data collection to publication
  • Transparency and open data as a non-negotiable principle
  • Technological innovation with an environmental purpose
  • Measurable socio-environmental impact, documented and verifiable
  • Institutional and interdisciplinary collaboration
  • Talent development and human-capital formation
  • Open, reproducible science

Unique Value Proposition

LAPIG is the only research lab that has spent 30 years bridging cutting-edge remote sensing science with the systematic production of open geospatial data platforms — translating research into operational tools for policymakers, sustainable agriculture, carbon markets, and the global scientific community. From Goiania to 17 countries.

02 — Logo

Logo System

The LAPIG logo pairs a typographic wordmark with an earth globe integrated into the letter "i". It has been refined for legibility at every scale.

Concept

The LAPIG wordmark uses bold geometric type with rounded corners and wide proportions, conveying scientific solidity and technological modernity. The earth globe replaces the dot on the "i", organically weaving the lab's geospatial scope into the typographic identity. The globe highlights South America, anchoring the brand in Brazil while projecting global reach. The diagonal green bar beneath the wordmark adds dynamism and grounds the composition.

!

Official logo is untouchable

The original files in Logos Lapig/ and Logo Lapig em inglês/ are the only official logos. Do not recreate, redesign, or alter any element of the logotype. All applications must use the original SVG/PNG files.

05 — Graphic Elements

Contour Lines and Hexagons

LAPIG's supporting visual language uses two graphic elements with distinct roles: topographic contour lines as the primary background element (textures, slide backgrounds, card backgrounds) and hexagons as a complementary element for image clipping, title icons, and frames. These elements never replace or compete with the official logotype.

Hexagonal Grid

The hexagon is the base geometric form of the visual system. It directly references geospatial grids (H3), data analysis, and scientific structure. Applications: background patterns (low opacity), photo frames, information containers, icons.

Hexagonal pattern · 10-15% opacity · light background

Contour Lines — Primary Background Element

Topographic contour lines are the primary graphic element for backgrounds and textures across all brand materials. They represent the landscape, topography, and terrain relief — a direct reference to cartography and remote sensing. Lines flow edge to edge with organic undulation and variable spacing, simulating a real topographic map.

!

Absolute rule: no line may overlap another

Lines must always be parallel, maintaining minimum distance between them. They never cross, never touch. This rule is inviolable and reproduces the real behavior of topographic isolines.

Gold contour lines · dark background · pronounced curves · lines never cross

Hexagons — Complementary Element

The hexagon is a complementary element, used exclusively for image clipping, photo frames (team, speakers), title icons, and information containers. A direct reference to geospatial grids (H3). Must not be used as a background pattern.

Usage Rules

✓

Contour lines as primary background

Use on slide backgrounds, cards, banners, and printed materials. Opacity between 18% and 32%. Stroke weight: 0.8 px to 1.5 px. Colors: green (#429B4D) or gold (#C4933F).

✓

Hexagons as clipping and framing

Team portraits, instructors, and speakers may use hexagonal crop. Title icons and seals may use hexagonal shape.

✓

Hexagons as icon containers

Stickers, favicons, and avatars may use the hexagon as a base shape.

✕

Do not use hexagons as background

The hexagonal grid must not be used as a background texture. Backgrounds belong exclusively to contour lines.

✕

Never cross contour lines

No line may overlap or cross another. Contour lines must always be parallel with variable spacing, like real topographic isolines.

✕

Do not mix with other patterns

Contour lines and hexagons are the only approved graphic elements. Do not combine with circles, triangles, or square grids.

Iconography

Style: Line icons, 1.5 px stroke weight, rounded corners (2 px radius).

Recommended library: Lucide Icons (MIT license, consistent with the geometric aesthetic). Customize as needed while keeping the same grid and stroke weight.

Grid: 24 x 24 px base with a 2 px bleed area. Uniform 1.5 px stroke.

Photography

Institutional photos: Favor natural light. Show the team in work context (field, lab, screen with map). Avoid staged poses.

Satellite imagery: May be used as a background with a dark overlay (70-85% opacity in Deep Forest) for text legibility.

Cerrado landscapes: Golden ipe trees, pasturelands, native vegetation — these form the priority visual archive. Always credit the photographer.

07 — Sub-brands

Sub-brand Policy

LAPIG operates multiple products, projects, and programs. Each may carry its own identity, provided it respects the master-brand hierarchy.

Hierarchy

TierTypeExamplesVisual Rule
MasterParent brandLAPIGPrimary logo. Present on every piece of collateral.
EndorsedOwned productAtlas das Pastagens, TVI, PastoLegal, CanopeiaOwn logo + "by LAPIG" lockup or LAPIG logo adjacent. May use a product-specific palette within the system.
ProgramProgram / seriesGeoCursos, LAPIG Connect, LAPIG Escola, LapigoodsCarries "LAPIG" in the name. Follows the master palette. May have its own icon (within the hexagonal grid).
ProjectExternal projectMapBiomas, Global Pasture Watch, Time2GrazeCo-branding. LAPIG logo appears as a partner in the logo bar. Does not alter the external project's identity.
EventOne-off eventRemanescer, Biomas BrasileirosMay have its own thematic identity. LAPIG logo in the organizer / supporter bar.

Co-branding Rules

LAPIG + UFG: The UFG logo must always accompany LAPIG on formal institutional materials. Position: to the right of LAPIG, separated by a vertical rule or a clear-space equivalent gap. The IESA logo, when present, sits between LAPIG and UFG.

LAPIG + Partners: On joint-project materials, all logos must share the same height. Arrange them in a horizontal bar at the bottom of the layout. LAPIG must not be disproportionately prominent, nor reduced below the minimum size.

09 — Governance

Brand Management

Approval rules, versioning, and brand-guardian contact information.

Approval Checklist

Every piece of collateral that uses the LAPIG brand must pass the following checks before publication:

  • ☐ Logo in the correct version (with/without subtitle, PT/EN) and within the clear-space zone
  • ☐ Colors match the official palette (no approximations)
  • ☐ Typography from the system families (Exo 2, DM Sans, JetBrains Mono)
  • ☐ Correct language for the context (see Chapter 08)
  • ☐ UFG co-branding present on formal institutional materials
  • ☐ WCAG AA contrast met on every text/background combination
  • ☐ Quantitative data verified and source cited
  • ☐ Brand-guardian approval obtained for external-facing materials
